Civic Leadership

Rooted in Georgia. Engaged Globally.

Jonathan "JT" Wu bridges public service and private sector expertise to build stronger institutions and create meaningful impact at the intersection of capital and community.

Jonathan "JT" Wu is a trusted strategic finance advisor and civic entrepreneur with deep expertise in mergers & acquisitions, corporate strategy, and cross-sector leadership. He has advised founders, C-Suite executives, policymakers, and capital partners through moments of strategic inflection, guiding hundreds of millions of dollars in transaction value across private equity, healthcare, and mission-driven growth sectors.

Through his advisory platform, Magnolia Prospect, JT provides white-glove support to business owners, family offices, and civic institutions. His services span sell-side M&A execution, capital raises, fractional CFO work, and complex transaction structuring. Drawing on senior experience in investment banking, leveraged finance, and private equity–backed corporate development, JT combines sophisticated financial modeling with pragmatic execution, serving as a fractional executive, transaction lead, or embedded partner depending on client needs.

Outside of the boardroom, JT is a nationally recognized civic leader and public-private strategist. He serves as the elected Chairman of Georgia's largest public library system, where his leadership has generated transformational growth and more than 300% annual returns on public investment. He is also the Founder and Executive Director of The Preface Project, an award-winning early childhood literacy nonprofit that trains high school Ambassadors to mentor young children nationwide through a proprietary multilingual platform. His work has influenced education and economic policy at the local and federal levels, including liaising with the U.S. State Department on international development initiatives.

A frequent speaker on the intersection of markets, public policy, and impact, JT's insights have been featured in Bloomberg International, NPR, The Atlanta Journal-Constitution, and La Repubblica. He has been recognized as one of the Top 25 Most Influential Asian Americans in Georgia and is a member of the Council of the United States and Italy.

JT graduated cum laude with an A.B. in Public and International Affairs from Princeton University, where he was a varsity coxswain on the heavyweight rowing team. He continues to serve on multiple civic and professional boards across the United States.
